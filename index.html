Why Gold Coast?

Gold Coast, the city of surf and skyscrapers

Legend has it that the Gold Coast earned its name in the 1950s, when visitors were drawn to its golden beaches and the promise of a sun-soaked paradise. What was once a quiet stretch of coastal towns and sugarcane fields soon transformed into a glittering destination — a place where surf, sand, and skyline meet.

Today, traces of the old Gold Coast still linger beneath its modern shine. Beyond the high-rise hotels and luxury boutiques, you’ll find beachside cafés serving fresh local coffee, surf clubs echoing with the sound of waves and laughter, and national parks where the rainforest meets the sea. From the golden curve of Burleigh Heads to the lush hinterland of Tamborine Mountain, the Gold Coast is a city of contrasts — where nature and glamour live side by side, and the sun always seems to linger a little longer on the horizon.
